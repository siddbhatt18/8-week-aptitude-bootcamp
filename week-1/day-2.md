### **Section A: Basic Divisibility Rules (2, 3, 4, 5, 6)**

#### **Rule 1: Divisibility by 2**
```
Rule: Last digit is even (0, 2, 4, 6, 8)
Speed Check: Look only at unit digit
```

**Examples:**
- 4,738 ‚Üí Last digit 8 (even) ‚Üí ‚úì Divisible by 2
- 9,651 ‚Üí Last digit 1 (odd) ‚Üí ‚úó Not divisible by 2

#### **Rule 2: Divisibility by 3**
```
Rule: Sum of all digits is divisible by 3
Speed Trick: Keep adding digits until single digit
```

**Example:** Is 8,745 divisible by 3?
```
8 + 7 + 4 + 5 = 24
2 + 4 = 6
6 √∑ 3 = 2 ‚Üí ‚úì Divisible by 3
```

#### **Rule 3: Divisibility by 4**
```
Rule: Last two digits form a number divisible by 4
Alternative: If tens digit is even, unit digit must be 0, 4, 8
           If tens digit is odd, unit digit must be 2, 6
```

**Examples:**
- 7,316 ‚Üí Last two digits: 16 ‚Üí 16 √∑ 4 = 4 ‚Üí ‚úì Divisible
- 5,234 ‚Üí Last two digits: 34 ‚Üí 34 √∑ 4 = 8.5 ‚Üí ‚úó Not divisible

**Quick Mental Check Pattern:**
```
00, 04, 08, 12, 16, 20, 24, 28, 32, 36, 40, 44, 48,
52, 56, 60, 64, 68, 72, 76, 80, 84, 88, 92, 96
```

#### **Rule 4: Divisibility by 5**
```
Rule: Last digit is 0 or 5
Speed Check: Instant - just check unit digit
```

#### **Rule 5: Divisibility by 6**
```
Rule: Divisible by both 2 AND 3
Process: Check rule 2 first (faster), then rule 3
```

**Example:** Is 7,842 divisible by 6?
```
Step 1: Last digit 2 (even) ‚Üí ‚úì Divisible by 2
Step 2: 7+8+4+2 = 21 ‚Üí 2+1 = 3 ‚Üí ‚úì Divisible by 3
Result: ‚úì Divisible by 6
```

### **Section B: Advanced Divisibility Rules (7, 8, 9, 10)**


### **Section C: Special Divisibility Rules (11, 12, 13)**

#### **Rule 10: Divisibility by 11**
```
Rule: (Sum of odd-positioned digits) - (Sum of even-positioned digits) = 0 or multiple of 11
Position counting: Start from right as position 1
```

**Example:** Is 9,53,847 divisible by 11?
```
Positions:  6  5  4  3  2  1
Number:     9  5  3  8  4  7

Odd positions (1,3,5): 7 + 8 + 5 = 20
Even positions (2,4,6): 4 + 3 + 9 = 16
Difference: 20 - 16 = 4 ‚Üí ‚úó Not divisible by 11
```

**Quick Pattern for 11:**
- Palindromes with even digits (1221, 135531) always divisible by 11
- Numbers like 121, 1331, 14641 are powers of 11

#### **Rule 11: Divisibility by 12**
```
Rule: Divisible by both 3 AND 4
Process: Check divisibility by 4 first (easier)
```

#### **Rule 12: Divisibility by 13**
```
Method: Add 4 times the last digit to remaining number
Repeat until you get a recognizable multiple of 13
```

**Example:** Is 2,197 divisible by 13?
```
219 + (7√ó4) = 219 + 28 = 247
24 + (7√ó4) = 24 + 28 = 52
52 √∑ 13 = 4 ‚Üí ‚úì Divisible by 13
```
